# Data provenance and reuse

## Scope

This repository contains cleaned, aggregate country-level analytic derivatives. It does not contain individual-level, identifiable, patient-level or restricted microdata. The MIT License applies only to repository code and original documentation; it does not relicense third-party source data.

## Provider conditions

- **WHO HIDR/HEAT, GHO and GHE:** WHO permits use, extraction, distribution and inclusion of its datasets in other products for public-health purposes, subject to attribution, retained rights, restrictions on third-party materials, no endorsement and the current WHO data terms: https://www.who.int/about/policies/publishing/data-policy/terms-and-conditions.
- **IHME/GBD-linked outputs:** use is subject to IHME terms and the applicable GBD release conditions. The repository provides only the cleaned values needed to reproduce this non-commercial analysis: https://gbdcollaboratorportal.healthdata.org/home/terms/.
- **World Bank:** Open Data are generally available under CC BY 4.0 unless indicator metadata specify otherwise. Attribution and no-endorsement conditions apply: https://data.worldbank.org/summary-terms-of-use.
- **Global Data Lab:** indicators may be used, adapted and shared for non-commercial purposes with source, link, data and version attribution; commercial use requires written permission: https://globaldatalab.org/termsofuse/.
- **Natural Earth:** map data are public domain: https://www.naturalearthdata.com/about/terms-of-use/.

IARC/GLOBOCAN, CanScreen5, CONCORD and GTSS/GATS were used for targeted source verification only. Their record-level data are not redistributed here.

## Attribution

Users should cite the associated manuscript, this repository and each original provider relevant to the variables reused. Indicator-level source names, units, years and notes are in `data/source_indicator_dictionary.csv`; access dates and terms are in `data/source_access_manifest.csv`.

## Interpretation limits

The data support ecological surveillance and reproducibility checks. They do not support individual risk inference, clinical prediction, causal attribution, national performance ranking or de-anonymisation.
